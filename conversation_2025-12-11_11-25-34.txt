Max: My brain’s been chewing on a few things lately:

1. How much of me is just a bunch of patterns I happened into.
   It’s like on the one hand, yeah, humans are probably all just code and experience. On the other, it really does “feel like” something to be me, and that feeling doesn’t totally track with a physics-and-chemistry description. Philosophy makes that space really obvious—computer science makes the pattern part just as obvious.
2. Alignment, but for humans systems not robots. 
   We talk so much about aligning AIs, but human systems aren’t even aligning humans. There’s incentives versus actual values, people stuck under broad rules nobody checks in on, whole systems that started with purpose but drifted away from it. It’s slightly weird to hand-wring about AI alignment while worrying if the job you’re supposed to “align” is even alignment-proof to begin with.
3. Attention Budget as a way to look at whole days.
   My mood, even my whole identity, is probably just whatever I gave my most attention to. Doomscrolling makes me anxious; specific songs clear my head; some books slow time down, others speed it up. Just tracking it all with a little attention budget is a simple way to notice what else I could have chosen instead.
